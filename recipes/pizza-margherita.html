Pizza Margherita Recipe

Description

Pizza Margherita is a timeless classic that celebrates simplicity and fresh ingredients. With a thin, crisp crust as the base, it's topped with tangy tomato sauce, creamy fresh mozzarella, and fragrant basil leaves. Baked until bubbly and golden, this Neapolitan-style pizza delivers balanced flavor with every bite — fresh, savory, and utterly satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (San Marzano or crushed tomatoes with a pinch of salt)
  • 4-6 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1-2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • All-purpose flour (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven - Heat oven to 475°F (245°C) or as high as it will go. If using a pizza stone, preheat it as well.
  2. Roll out the dough - On a floured surface, stretch or roll the dough into a 10-12 inch circle. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet or pizza peel.
  3. Add sauce - Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  4. Add cheese - Scatter torn mozzarella pieces over the sauce.
  5. Bake the pizza - Bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.
  6. Finish and serve - Remove from oven, top with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve hot.
