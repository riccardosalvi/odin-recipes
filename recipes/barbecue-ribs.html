Barbecue Ribs Recipe

Description

These barbecue ribs are fall-off-the-bone tender, coated in a smoky dry rub, and finished with a sticky, caramelized BBQ glaze. Cooked low and slow, they’re packed with deep, savory flavor and that perfect balance of sweet, tangy, and smoky. Whether you're grilling outside or baking in the oven, these ribs bring true barbecue flavor to your table—no smoker required.

Ingredients

  • 2 racks of pork ribs (baby back or spare ribs)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin (optional, for smoky depth)
  • 1-1½ cups barbecue sauce (your favorite brand or homemade)

Steps

  1. Prep the ribs - Remove the thin membrane from the back of the ribs for tenderness. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Make the dry rub - In a bowl, mix brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and optional spices.
  3. Season the ribs - Rub the spice mixture all over both sides of the ribs. Let them rest for 15-30 minutes.
  4. Bake low and slow - Wrap ribs in foil and bake at 300°F (150°C) for 2.5 to 3 hours, until tender.
  5. Glaze and grill (or broil) - Unwrap the ribs, brush with BBQ sauce, and either:
    • Grill for 5-10 minutes over medium heat, flipping once, or
    • Broil in the oven for 3-5 minutes, until the sauce bubbles and caramelizes.
  6. Rest and serve - Let ribs rest for 5-10 minutes. Slice between the bones and serve with extra sauce.
